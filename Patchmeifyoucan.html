About the Project

This is a fast-paced first-person shooter where you play as a voodoo tailor on a mission to stitch back together and purify your runaway creations, now scattered across eerie amusement park attractions. The setting blends the bizarre and the whimsical, creating a unique atmosphere that sets the tone for a fresh and stylized FPS experience.

The gameplay features high-speed, fluid movement inspired by classic titles like Ape Escape. Players are encouraged to stay mobile, chaining actions quickly while navigating in levels filled with misbehaving creations. This fast-paced rhythm keeps the action engaging and constantly evolving.

Thanks to the dedication and synergy of the entire team, we were able to deliver a fun experience. Each member contributed with passion and creativity, allowing us to craft a game that not only plays well but also offers a distinct visual and thematic identity.

This academic project was also a major opportunity for personal growth. I focused particularly on combat design, and more specifically on the development of shooting systems — refining weapon mechanics, player feedback, and pacing to support the game’s energetic feel.

Combat Design Breakdown

Weapon Core Mechanics

The stitching gun combines ranged combat with melee finishing moves.

  • Fire your needle at medium range.The player has a single projectile (a needle); once fired, they must wait for the needle to return to the weapon before firing again.
  • The player can shoot while sliding, jumping, or crouching.
  • Once the needle hits and attaches to an enemy, the player must repeatedly press the "E" key to pull the enemy toward them.
  • When firing or hooking an enemy, the player is slowed until the needle returns to the rifle or for the duration of the hook
  • if the player misses his shot the player is slowed until the needle returns and the weapon is re-primed.

Enemy Design

Each enemy type has unique behaviors and weak points:

  • Puppets wander around the map
  • Puppets have two conical fields of view.Both cones extend degrees vertically and horizontally.
  • Each puppet has a spherical hearing range with a radius
  • If a standing player enters this hearing radius, the puppet will turn toward them and run away.
  • While escaping, puppets will dynamically use tunnels, obstacles, and sudden jumps/turns to increase distance from the player.

    • The enemy design is inspired from the game " ape escape"

Feedback Systems

Clear feedback reinforces player actions:

  • Visual hit text showing that the enemy is locked on your needle
  • Audio cues for successful hits
  • Visual effect when you're crouching
  • Skull eye color changing when hitting an enemy or when enemy are close
  • Visual effect on top of the enemy model when they spots you
